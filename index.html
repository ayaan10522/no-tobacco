About No Tobacco

No Tobacco is a global campaign dedicated to raising awareness about the dangers of tobacco use and providing support for those who want to quit. Our mission is to create a healthier world by reducing tobacco consumption and promoting smoke-free environments.

We believe that by educating people about the harmful effects of tobacco and offering resources to help quit, we can significantly reduce the number of tobacco-related diseases and deaths worldwide.

Dangers of Tobacco

Health Risks

  • Increased risk of cancer, especially lung cancer, but also mouth, throat, esophagus, pancreas, bladder, cervix, and kidney cancer
  • Heart disease, including heart attacks and strokes
  • Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) such as chronic bronchitis and emphysema
  • Respiratory issues including asthma exacerbations and chronic respiratory symptoms
  • Adverse effects on pregnancy, including increased risk of miscarriage, preterm birth, and low birth weight
  • Weakened immune system, making it harder to fight off infections
  • Reduced fertility in both men and women

Environmental Impact

  • Deforestation for tobacco cultivation
  • Pollution from cigarette butts, which are the most common form of litter
  • Air pollution from smoke and secondhand smoke exposure
  • Use of pesticides and chemicals in tobacco farming that harm the environment

Tips to Quit Tobacco

Prepare to Quit

  1. Set a quit date and make a plan
  2. Identify your triggers and plan how to avoid them
  3. Tell friends, family, and coworkers about your plan to quit

During the Quit Process

  1. Use nicotine replacement therapy or prescribed medications
  2. Stay active to manage cravings and stress
  3. Drink water and eat healthy snacks to keep your mouth busy
  4. Avoid situations where you are tempted to smoke
  5. Seek support from friends, family, or support groups
  6. Practice deep breathing and relaxation techniques

Stay Tobacco-Free

  1. Celebrate your successes and reward yourself
  2. Stay vigilant about triggers and have a plan to deal with cravings
  3. Continue to seek support if needed
  4. Stay positive and focus on the benefits of a tobacco-free life
